# NAVLYS PRESS EMBARGO — HOW TO USE

*How to use a press embargo to amplify the 31 May 2026 launch.*

## WHAT IS A PRESS EMBARGO?

An embargo is an agreement between a source (NAVLYS) and a journalist. The journalist receives information in advance but commits to publishing only after a **specific date and time** ("embargo lift"). In exchange, they prepare their piece calmly and can publish exactly at launch moment.

## WHY USE ONE FOR NAVLYS?

- **Media concentration:** ten pieces on the same day weigh more than ten pieces spread over three weeks.
- **Quality preparation:** journalists have time to dig, call, test the calculator, quote.
- **Discovery effect:** the public discovers NAVLYS through several channels simultaneously.

## WHEN TO USE IT

✅ **Recommended embargo lift:** **Sunday 31 May 2026 at 23:00 Paris time (21:00 UTC)**

- Why Sunday night? Because economic newsrooms close Sunday evening for Monday morning. Monday print editions can headline on NAVLYS, and websites publish at midnight.
- Monday morning, radio takes over. TV picks up by midday.

## RULES TO RESPECT ON NAVLYS' SIDE

- ⚠️ **Never send the full press kit without a formally accepted embargo.**
- Always keep a written trace (journalist's acknowledgment email).
- Don't send to more than 30 people under embargo: the larger the group, the higher the leak risk.
- Have a plan B: if embargo breaks (rare but possible), immediately publish the official release on brunopartouche.com and release all other journalists from their embargo.

## RULES IF A JOURNALIST REFUSES THE EMBARGO

Some refuse on principle (notably Anglo-Saxon press). In that case:
- Don't insist.
- Offer to wait for D0 and receive the press kit at the lift.
- Maintain a cordial relationship.

## NAVLYS LAUNCHES FRANCE'S FIRST DAILY FINANCIAL "COMPASS": A FREE, INDEPENDENT EDUCATIONAL PLATFORM WITH NO RETURN PROMISES

**Marseille, 31 May 2026** — NAVLYS, a financial education platform founded by Bruno Mark Partouche, a French Investment Adviser (CIF, registered with ORIAS), is opening to the public today. Its mission: to give French households a clear bearing on their money — without selling a product and without promising returns.

NAVLYS offers a portfolio allocation simulator, a hidden-fee calculator, and a daily "compass of the day" — a single, short, educational publication released every morning as a quiet reference point for savers overwhelmed by the constant financial noise.

**The context is straightforward.** French households hold nearly €6 trillion of savings, the majority of which sits in low-yielding savings books and euro funds. On the active investor side, France's market regulator AMF has repeatedly noted that most retail investors do not know exactly how much they pay in annual fees. That blind spot is precisely what NAVLYS attacks.

**"We don't promise wealth. We promise a bearing,"** says founder Bruno Mark Partouche. *"I spent years on the Mediterranean. At sea, without a bearing, you drift. With ten bearings, you go in circles. With one clear bearing, you move forward. Personal finance is the same. NAVLYS is one bearing per day. Not ten."*

NAVLYS operates within a strict regulatory framework. Bruno Mark Partouche is a French CIF registered with ORIAS, affiliated with an AMF-approved association. The NAVLYS trademark is registered with INPI. No capital ties to any broker, bank or asset manager.

The business model rests on premium educational content and one-to-one advisory delivered via brunopartouche.com — while the simulator and the daily compass remain free and open to all.

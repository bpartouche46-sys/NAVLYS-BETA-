# BRUNO MARK PARTOUCHE BIO — LONG (≈ 500 WORDS)

Bruno Mark Partouche is a French finance and wealth advisory professional, founder of the educational brand NAVLYS, and operates personally as a French Investment Adviser (CIF), registered with ORIAS. Based in Marseille on the Mediterranean coast that he sailed for years as a semi-professional skipper, he embodies a distinctive approach to personal finance: rigorous, educational, and patient.

His path is deliberately cross-disciplinary. On one side, classical financial expertise: private insurance, life insurance, wealth allocation, fee analysis, long-term structuring, advisory work with retail savers and entrepreneurs. On the other, a culture of care and calm built in parallel: trumpet player, practitioner of reflexology, reiki, acupressure and Tibetan singing bowls. This dual competence is not a biographical detail — it is the matrix of his method. *"On a sailboat as in a financial life, what matters is not point-in-time speed, it's the trajectory,"* he likes to say.

His years lived at sea left a lasting mark. Maritime vocabulary — bearing, sap, drift, fair wind, slack water — runs through the language of NAVLYS. To him, the contemporary French saver resembles a navigator without a bearing: surrounded by permanent financial noise — finfluencers, alerts, FOMO, promises of doubling, contradictory signals — yet drifting. NAVLYS was born from this observation: France lacked an independent educational landmark, with no hidden sales, no retrocession, no return promise.

Bruno Mark Partouche's commitment is explicitly deontological. As a registered CIF, he is affiliated with an AMF-approved association, complies with the transparency and advisory-duty obligations specific to the status, and conducts his personalised advisory through his institutional site **brunopartouche.com**. The NAVLYS brand, registered with INPI, is deliberately separate from his personalised advisory: NAVLYS sells no financial product, receives no commission, displays no third-party advertising. That boundary is claimed.

Beyond the numbers, Bruno Mark Partouche defends a broader idea: finance is not a competition, it's a practice. Like music, like reflexology, like sailing: it is built over time, through small repeated gestures, and tolerates haste poorly. The "daily compass" published by NAVLYS — a single, short publication with no aggressive notification — is the direct expression of this philosophy: one landmark per day, not ten.

Available for interviews in French and English, by video, phone or in person in Marseille or Paris, Bruno Mark Partouche speaks willingly on topics of financial education, hidden fees, wealth allocation, saver psychology, and more broadly on the ethics of independent financial advisory in France.

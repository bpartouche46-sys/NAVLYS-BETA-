## CHAPTER ONE — GENERAL

**1. Definitions and interpretation.**
1.1 In these Articles: "the **Company**" — NAVLYS WEB SERVICE LTD; "the **Law**" —
the Israeli Companies Law, 5759-1999, as amended, and the regulations
thereunder; "**Office Holder**" — as defined in the Law; "**Shareholder**" — a
shareholder of record of the Company; "**writing**" — includes electronic means.
1.2 Words importing the singular include the plural and vice versa; any term
not defined herein shall bear the meaning given to it in the Law.
1.3 In the event of a contradiction between these Articles and a mandatory
provision of the Law, the provision of the Law shall prevail; where the Law
permits stipulation, these Articles shall prevail.

**2. Name.** The name of the Company is:
in Hebrew — נבליס ווב סרוויס בע"מ; in English — NAVLYS WEB SERVICE LTD.

**3. Objects.**
3.1 Pursuant to Section 32(1) of the Law, the objects of the Company are to
engage in **any lawful occupation**.
3.2 Without derogating from the generality of Article 3.1, the Company's
principal intended fields of activity are:
(a) research and development (R&D) of software, artificial-intelligence
systems, algorithms and digital technologies;
(b) development, operation and provision of Software-as-a-Service (SaaS),
including subscription applications and services based on software and voice;
(c) creation, ownership and exploitation of intangible assets (software,
platforms, trademarks, data, models) and the grant of licenses thereover;
(d) hosting, technical operation, maintenance and security of digital platforms;
(e) marketing of subscriptions, educational and informational content and
related digital services, in Israel and abroad.

**4. Limited liability.** The liability of each Shareholder for the Company's
obligations is limited to payment of the amount, if any, unpaid on the shares
held by such Shareholder (Section 35 of the Law).

**5. Private company.** The Company is a private company; accordingly:
5.1 the number of its shareholders shall not exceed fifty (50), excluding
employees and former employees;
5.2 the Company shall not offer its securities to the public;
5.3 any transfer of shares is subject to Article 12 below.

## CHAPTER TWO — SHARE CAPITAL AND SHARES

**6. Authorized share capital.** The authorized share capital of the Company is
**NIS 10,000**, divided into **1,000,000 ordinary shares of a nominal value of
NIS 0.01 each** (the "**Ordinary Shares**").

**7. Rights attached to shares.** Each Ordinary Share confers upon its holder:
(a) one vote at every general meeting; (b) an equal right to dividends and
other distributions; (c) an equal right to participate in the distribution of
surplus assets upon winding-up; (d) all other rights conferred by the Law.

**8. Issuance of shares.** The board of directors may issue shares and other
securities up to the limit of the authorized capital, at such consideration and
on such terms as it deems fit, and may grant options and other rights over
shares.

**9. Initial allotment.** Upon incorporation, **100 Ordinary Shares** are
allotted, fully paid, to **Bruno Mark Partouche** (100 % of the issued capital).

**10. Share certificates.** A Shareholder is entitled, upon request, to one
certificate evidencing the shares registered in the Shareholder's name, signed
in accordance with Article 22.

**11. Register of shareholders.** The Company shall maintain a register of
shareholders in accordance with the Law; entry therein constitutes prima facie
evidence of ownership.

**12. Transfer of shares.**
12.1 Shares may be transferred by a deed of transfer signed by the transferor
and the transferee, delivered to the Company together with the share
certificate (if issued).
12.2 Any transfer of shares requires the **prior approval of the board of
directors**, which may decline to register a transfer at its discretion, for
the good of the Company; so long as the Company has a sole shareholder who is
also the sole director, that shareholder's written decision constitutes such
approval.
12.3 No transfer shall be registered which would cause the Company to cease
qualifying as a private company.

**13. Transmission by operation of law.** Upon the death or incapacity of a
Shareholder, the persons entitled under law (heirs, administrators, guardians)
shall be recognized as holders of the shares upon delivery of proper proof, and
may elect to be registered as shareholders or to transfer the shares in
accordance with Article 12.

**14. Changes to capital.** The general meeting may, by ordinary resolution:
increase the authorized capital; consolidate, sub-divide, cancel or reduce
capital in accordance with the Law; and create classes of shares.

## CHAPTER THREE — GENERAL MEETINGS

**15. Sole shareholder.** So long as the Company has a single Shareholder, that
Shareholder holds all the powers of the general meeting, and a **decision in
writing signed by the sole Shareholder** has the force of a resolution of the
general meeting (Section 62 of the Law); no prior notice, quorum or meeting is
required.

**16. Annual and special meetings.** If the Company has more than one
Shareholder: an annual general meeting shall be held in accordance with the
Law; the board may convene a special meeting at any time and shall do so at the
demand of those entitled under Section 63 of the Law.

**17. Notice, quorum, voting.**
17.1 Notice of a general meeting shall be given at least **7 days** in advance
(all Shareholders may waive notice in writing).
17.2 A quorum is one or more Shareholders holding at least **51 %** of the
voting rights, present in person, by proxy or by means of communication.
17.3 Resolutions of the general meeting are adopted by an **ordinary majority**
of the votes cast, unless the Law requires otherwise.
17.4 A resolution in writing signed by all Shareholders entitled to vote is
valid as if adopted at a duly convened meeting.
17.5 Meetings may be held by any means of communication allowing all
participants to hear one another simultaneously.

## CHAPTER FOUR — THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS

**18. Composition.** The board shall consist of **one (1) or more directors**.
The Company may lawfully have a **sole director**. The first director is
**Bruno Mark Partouche**.

**19. Appointment and removal.** Directors are appointed and removed by the
general meeting (or by written decision of the sole Shareholder), effective as
stated in the resolution. A director may appoint an alternate, subject to the Law.

**20. Powers.** The board shall outline the Company's policy and supervise the
performance of the general manager; it holds every power of the Company not
conferred by the Law or these Articles upon another organ. So long as no
general manager has been appointed, the board shall also hold the powers of
general management (Section 121 of the Law).

**21. Proceedings of the board.**
21.1 While the Company has a sole director, that director's **written decision**
constitutes a resolution of the board, without notice or meeting.
21.2 With more than one director: any director may convene a meeting on
reasonable notice; the quorum is a majority of directors; resolutions are
adopted by simple majority, the chair having no casting vote; meetings may be
held by any means of communication; a unanimous written resolution is valid as
a board resolution.
21.3 Minutes shall be kept in accordance with the Law and, once signed by the
chair of the meeting, constitute prima facie evidence of their content.

**22. Signatory rights.** The Company shall be bound by the signature of the
person(s) empowered thereto by the board, together with the Company's name
(stamp or print). **First signatory: Bruno Mark Partouche, signing alone,
without limitation of amount.**

**23. General manager.** The board may appoint a general manager (CEO) and
define the terms of office. The same person may serve simultaneously as
director and general manager (in a private company, without special approval
requirements applicable to public companies).

## CHAPTER FIVE — OFFICE HOLDERS: EXEMPTION, INDEMNIFICATION, INSURANCE

**24. Exemption.** The Company may, to the fullest extent permitted by
Sections 258–259 of the Law, exempt an Office Holder in advance from liability
toward the Company for damage caused by breach of the **duty of care** — except
a breach of the duty of care in a Distribution (as defined in the Law).

**25. Indemnification.** The Company may indemnify an Office Holder,
retroactively or by advance undertaking, to the fullest extent permitted by
Sections 260–261 of the Law, including for: (a) monetary liability imposed in
favor of another person by judgment (including settlement or arbitral award
approved by court) — provided the advance undertaking is limited to categories
of events foreseeable in the board's opinion and to a reasonable amount or
criteria set by the board; (b) reasonable litigation expenses, including
attorneys' fees, in proceedings as specified in the Law; (c) any other matter
for which indemnification is or shall be permitted by law.

**26. Insurance.** The Company may insure the liability of an Office Holder to
the fullest extent permitted by law, including for breach of the duty of care
toward the Company or another person, breach of the duty of loyalty toward the
Company committed in good faith with reasonable grounds to believe the act
would not harm the Company, and monetary liability imposed in favor of another
person.

**27. Statutory limits.** No exemption, indemnification or insurance shall be
granted for: (a) breach of the **duty of loyalty**, except as permitted by law
for insurance as set out in Article 26; (b) breach of the duty of care
committed **intentionally or recklessly**, other than mere negligence; (c) an
act intended to derive an unlawful personal profit; (d) a fine, civil fine,
financial sanction or forfeit imposed on the Office Holder. Articles 24–27
apply to the fullest extent permitted from time to time by the Law; an
amendment of the Law shall extend them automatically to the new permitted extent.

## CHAPTER SIX — DISTRIBUTIONS, RECORDS, AUDIT

**28. Dividends and distributions.** The board may resolve upon a Distribution
in accordance with the criteria of the Law (profit test and solvency test).
Dividends shall be distributed to Shareholders pro rata to the paid-up nominal
value of their shares. No interest accrues on dividends.

**29. Intellectual property.** Every intangible asset developed by the Company
or for it — by its Office Holders, employees or contractors within the scope of
its activity — is the **exclusive property of the Company**, and the Company
alone may license it. *(Foundation of the Preferred Technology Enterprise
regime: the preferred intangible asset must be owned by the Israeli enterprise.)*

**30. Books of account; auditor.** The Company shall keep books of account and
prepare financial statements in accordance with the Law, including **separate
analytical tracking of research-and-development expenditure**, and shall
appoint an auditing accountant (רואה חשבון מבקר) as required by the Law.

**31. Notices.** A notice by or to the Company may be delivered personally, by
registered mail, or by electronic means to the address recorded with the
Company, and shall be deemed served: upon delivery (in person); three (3)
business days after posting; or one (1) business day after electronic dispatch.

## CHAPTER SEVEN — FINAL PROVISIONS

**32. Amendment of these Articles.** The general meeting may amend these
Articles by **ordinary majority** (or by written decision of the sole
Shareholder).

**33. Merger and reorganization.** The Company may effect a merger,
arrangement or reorganization in accordance with the provisions of the Law.

**34. Winding-up.** Upon winding-up, the surplus assets shall be distributed
among the Shareholders pro rata to the paid-up nominal value of their shares.

**35. Governing law and jurisdiction.** These Articles are governed by the laws
of the State of Israel; the competent courts of the district of the Company's
registered office shall have exclusive jurisdiction.

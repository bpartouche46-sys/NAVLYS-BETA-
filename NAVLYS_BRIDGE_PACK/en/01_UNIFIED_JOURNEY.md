# NAVLYS — Unified Journey: 7 ports of call to reach your heading

> The bridge between your 3 modules. One voyage, seven ports, one complete plan in hand.

---

## The idea in one sentence

The visitor enters with "here is my dream", discovers how much their hidden fees really cost, then watches their heading get closer thanks to the 90/10 method. One journey, one story, one plan.

---

## Port 1 — What is your heading?

**What the visitor does:** picks their dream from 8 ready-made buoys.

Image: eight colored lighthouses on the horizon, each lighting a different heading. Travel, car, wedding, birth, studies, real estate, retirement, unexpected. The visitor clicks on the lighthouse that calls them.

Input: target amount (€), annual expected return slider (1 to 8 %).

Exit: the heading is set. The ship knows where it goes.

---

## Port 2 — How long to get there?

**What the visitor does:** picks their sailing mode.

Image: three different sails. The cautious sail (long-term), the balanced sail (mid-term), the fast sail (short-term). Each sail changes the ship's speed.

Input: duration in months to reach the goal, editable.

Exit: a timeline is set. The ship knows its cruising speed.

---

## Port 3 — Where do you stand on fees?

**What the visitor does:** describes their current setup.

Image: the hull of the ship. If covered in barnacles (hidden fees), it slows down. The visitor names their current broker, bank, monthly trade volume.

Input: broker (slug), bank (slug), trades/month, average ticket €, monthly bank fees €.

Exit: the hull has been inspected. The visitor sees their barnacles.

---

## Port 4 — The revealed gap

**What the visitor does:** discovers the hidden cost.

Image: a tide recedes and reveals the rocks underwater. The calculation appears: "You pay X €/year in hidden fees. With an aligned setup, you would save Y €/year. These savings alone speed up your heading by Z months."

Exit: low tide has spoken. The visitor knows how much they lose.

---

## Port 5 — The 90/10 method

**What the visitor does:** discovers the strategy.

Image: a fortress on the main island (90 % of capital, long-term ETF), and a small sailing boat that goes out to explore (10 % in active play). The fortress never moves. The sailing boat may have high or low tides, but cannot threaten the fortress.

Exit: the philosophy is clear. Safety first, exploration next.

---

## Port 6 — Your unified plan

**What the visitor does:** sees their full plan on a single card.

The card shows:
- their heading (target amount)
- their accelerated timeline (months gained from fee savings + 90/10)
- the 90/10 split in real euros
- the maximum protection margin (the safety net)
- the default recommended strategy (half-Kelly — best ROI/safety trade-off)

Image: a personal nautical chart, with the heading, the route, the lighthouses.

Exit: the visitor holds their plan in hand.

---

## Port 7 — Receive your full plan

**What the visitor does:** saves their plan.

Image: a sealed bottle is handed to them. They slip in their email to receive it, or press the WhatsApp button to share with a loved one.

Input: email, GDPR consent.

Exit: the visitor leaves with their plan, by email or message. The educational disclaimer comes along: "Educational information. You remain the sole decision-maker. Test in simulation."

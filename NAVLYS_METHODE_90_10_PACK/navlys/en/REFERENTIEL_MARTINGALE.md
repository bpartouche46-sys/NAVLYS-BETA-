## 1. The idea in one sentence

You keep 90 % of your money safe inside the fortress. You only sail with 10 % out on the sea.

That's it. The rest is just method — how to grow that 10 % without ever touching the 90 %.

---

## 2. The maritime metaphor

Picture your wealth as an island.

On that island, you build a **fortress**. It shelters 90 % of your money. The walls are thick. Nobody gets in. Not even you on a bad day.

Next to the fortress, there is a small **harbour**. You own a **sailboat**. That boat carries 10 % of your money. You head out when the sea is calm. You return when it turns rough.

If one day the boat sinks, you only lose those 10 %. The fortress still stands. You can buy a new boat.

That is the NAVLYS principle. Safety first. Performance after. Never the other way around.

---

## 3. Why 90/10 and not 100/0 or 50/50 ?

**100/0** — Everything is safe. You sleep well. But your money never grows.

**50/50** — You play with half. If the sea rages, you lose half. That is too much.

**90/10** — You play just enough to make a difference. You protect the essential. That is the right balance.

The numbers confirm it. Across thousands of simulations, keeping 10 % at play earns almost as much as risking it all. But the risk is ten times smaller.

---

## 4. The science behind the sailboat

When you head out, you have a choice : how much sail do you raise ?

- Too much sail : you go fast but you can capsize.
- Too little sail : you do not move.
- Just enough : you go well and stay upright.

This "just enough" has a name : the **Kelly formula**.

`f* = (b × p − q) / b`

You do not need to understand the formula. Here is what it tells you :

- The surer your heading (strong signal), the more sail you can raise.
- The harsher the sea (weak signal), the less sail you carry.

Kelly calculates the exact sail for every trip. Without emotion.

## 5. Kelly vs Martingale — the simulation verdict

We tested three sailing styles across 10 000 simulated trips :

**Full Kelly** — You trim your sails to the wind.
- Average gain : **+17 %**
- Risk of total loss : **0 %**
- Image : the perfectly trimmed sail. You catch the wind without ever capsizing.

**Martingale** — When you fail a trip, you double the stake on the next one.
- Average gain : **+9.7 %**
- Risk of total loss : **37 %**
- Image : faster when it works. But 1 trip out of 3 ends with the sailor wrecked at port.

**Fixed stake** — You always raise the same sail.
- Average gain : **+3.6 %**
- Risk of total loss : **0 %**
- Image : slow, safe sailing. Decent, but flat.

**Verdict : Kelly wins.** More gain, zero wreck. It is the engine of the NAVLYS system.

## 6. The role of the signal — your heading

Without a heading, the boat drifts. Our tests proved it : a random signal (50/50) yields **−1.07 %** return. You lose.

With a quality signal (55 % accurate, which is modest), you reach **+17 %**. The difference does not come from the stake. It comes from the heading.

The signal is what the algorithm watches before sailing out : the tide of the market, the wind of news, the state of the sky. If all aligns, we sail. Otherwise, we stay at port.

**70.5 % profitable days** in the tests. 7 tides out of 10 are favourable.

## 7. The 5 screens of the system

Everything happens on your phone. 5 screens. Simple.

1. **Welcome** — You see your fortress and your sailboat. You know where you stand.
2. **Today's idea** — The algorithm tells you : sail out or stay at port, and why.
3. **My settings** — 5 simple buttons. You choose your prudence.
4. **Execute** — You confirm. The sailboat leaves.
5. **Recap** — In the evening, you see the trip. What you earned. What you learned.

No complicated chart. No jargon. One tide per day.

## 8. The 14 parameters — the engine room

Under the deck, there are 14 settings. You only see 5. The other 9 run on their own.

The 5 visible ones :
- Fortress share (default 90 %)
- Sailboat size (default 10 %)
- Prudence threshold (default medium)
- Trips per day (default 1)
- Auto or manual mode

The 9 hidden ones are technical settings. For the curious, a "Details" button reveals them. Otherwise, the algorithm tunes them for you.

## 9. What NAVLYS is not

It is not a casino. It is not a promise of wealth. It is not a magic robot.

It is a method. A methodical captain beats a lucky captain.

The 90 % fortress is your real safety. The 10 % sailboat is your playground. If the sea turns cruel, you return to port with a limited loss. You never sink.
